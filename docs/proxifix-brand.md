ProxiFix — Brand Voice Guide

Tone
- Friendly, helpful, and confident.
- Concise: use short sentences and active verbs.
- Clear calls-to-action: tell users what to do next.

Terminology
- Use `FixPoints` (capital F and P) for the credits system.
- Use `XP` (all caps) for experience points.
- Prefer the word "workers" in UI copy (use "fixers" only in marketing headlines when needed).
- Use "Get started" as primary sign-up CTA.

Buttons / CTAs
- Use short, action-first labels: "Get started", "Post a job", "Apply", "View", "Send proposal".
- Use Title Case for button labels.

Headings & Microcopy
- Be direct and helpful: e.g., "Find trusted local workers", "Create a job request", "Make your profile stand out".
- Empty states should be warm and actionable: "No jobs match your filters." or "Worker profiles will appear here once workers sign up.".

Examples
- Hero: "Find trusted local workers, reward great work, and earn FixPoints."
- Onboarding: "Join ProxiFix — start with FixPoints and build your XP profile." 

Notes
- Keep language consistent across templates and components.
- Prefer contractions where appropriate to sound natural (e.g., "You haven't applied yet").
